Timeline of the Renaissance

1300s
Early Renaissance begins in Italy, marked by developments in art, literature, and humanism.
1439
Johannes Gutenberg invents the printing press, revolutionizing the spread of knowledge.
1492
Christopher Columbus discovers the Americas, expanding European horizons.
1503
Leonardo da Vinci paints the Mona Lisa, one of the most famous paintings in history.
1517
Martin Luther posts his 95 Theses, sparking the Protestant Reformation.
1543
Nicolaus Copernicus publishes De revolutionibus orbium coelestium, proposing the heliocentric model of the universe.
1564
Birth of William Shakespeare, who would become one of the greatest playwrights of all time.
1590s
Galileo Galilei makes significant advancements in physics and astronomy.
1610
Galileo publishes his observations supporting the heliocentric theory.
1648
The Treaty of Westphalia ends the Thirty Years' War, signaling the decline of Renaissance-era political structures.
